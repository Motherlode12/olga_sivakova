Anyone who reads Old and Middle English literary texts will be familiar 
        with the mid-brown volumes of the EETS, with the symbol of Alfred's jewel
         embossed on the front cover. Most of the works attributed to King Alfred 
         or to Aelfric, along with some of those by bishop Wulfstan and much anonymous
          prose and verse from the pre-Conquest period, are to be found within 
          the Society's three series; all of the surviving medieval drama, most 
          of the Middle English romances, much religious and secular prose and
           verse including the English works of John Gower, Thomas Hoccleve 
           and most of Caxton's prints all find their place in the publications.
